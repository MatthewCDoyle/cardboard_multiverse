## Why concentration kills returns

In 2021, many collectors were heavily concentrated in one or two modern player positions — Joe Burrow, Ja Morant, Zion Williamson. When the market peaked and these players' cards crashed 60–80%, heavily concentrated collections lost the majority of their value.

Collectors who had diversified — vintage alongside modern, basketball alongside football, multiple players across the portfolio — experienced the same market conditions but held 30–40% of their peak value instead of 10–15%.

Diversification does not prevent losses. It limits the severity of the worst-case scenarios.

---

## The four rules and what they protect against

**Rule 1: No single card over 20% of total portfolio value**

A single card representing more than 20% of your collection creates a single-point failure. If that card suffers a sudden value drop — the player gets injured, a scandal emerges, or supply increases — more than a fifth of your portfolio is impacted simultaneously.

*What it protects against:* Card-specific events (authentication disputes, sudden resurgence of population, market liquidity crises for that specific card)

*How to apply:* Calculate every card's percentage of total portfolio quarterly. Any card over 20% should either be sold to rebalance or your total portfolio should be grown to bring it under the threshold.

**Rule 2: No single player over 30% of total portfolio value**

A player can have multiple cards — their rookie, parallels, autos — and each may be under 20% individually while the player as a whole represents 50% of your portfolio. This rule catches that aggregated concentration.

*What it protects against:* Player-specific events (career-ending injury, criminal charges, retirement announcement that triggers a market revaluation)

*How to apply:* Sum all cards for a single player. Divide by total portfolio value. If over 30%, reduce the player position.

**Rule 3: No single sport over 50% of total portfolio value**

Sports markets cycle differently. When NFL season is active (September–January), football card demand spikes. When NFL is in offseason, basketball and baseball absorb that collector energy. A portfolio balanced across sports is less sensitive to sport-specific cycles.

*What it protects against:* Sport-specific market cycles, league events (lockouts, rule changes, broadcast deal changes), generational shifts in sport popularity

*How to apply:* Sum all cards by sport. No single sport should exceed 50% of total value.

**Rule 4: No single era over 60% of total portfolio value**

The modern ultra-chrome era (2010–present) experienced the largest gains and largest crashes of any era. Vintage (pre-1990) held value significantly better in downturns but has lower ceiling upside. A portfolio balanced across eras captures the best of both.

*What it protects against:* Era-specific market corrections, technology changes in card production that affect an era's perceived value, generational shifts in collector preferences

*How to apply:* Categorize each card as vintage / junk-wax / modern / ultra-modern. Sum by category. No category should exceed 60%.

## A worked example: the $5,000 portfolio audit

Starting portfolio:
- Mahomes 2017 Prizm Silver RC PSA 10: $4,200 (84% of portfolio)
- 3 Mahomes parallels: $600 (12%)
- LeBron James Prizm: $200 (4%)

**Current violations:**
- Single card over 20%: YES — Mahomes RC at 84%
- Single player over 30%: YES — Mahomes at 96% of portfolio
- Single sport over 50%: YES — Football at 96%
- Single era over 60%: YES — Ultra-modern at 96%

**The rebalancing plan:**
This is a deeply concentrated portfolio. The correct action is not to sell the Mahomes RC — it is to grow the portfolio around it. Over 90 days, allocate new acquisitions exclusively to basketball, vintage, and non-Mahomes players until each rule is satisfied.

After adding $3,000 in basketball vintage (1986 Fleer), $1,500 in LeBron and Jordan Prizm cards, and $2,500 in football cards of other players, the portfolio rebalances:
- Mahomes RC: $4,200 of $11,500 = 36% (still concentrated — continue rebalancing)
- Mahomes total: $4,800 = 42% (improving)
- Football total: $5,800 = 50% (at limit)
- Ultra-modern: $6,200 = 54% (within rule)

Rebalancing is a direction, not a one-time event. Quarterly audits keep the portfolio within bounds as values shift.
