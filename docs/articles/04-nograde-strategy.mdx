## The capital trap most collectors don't see

Every dollar sitting in a nograde is a dollar that cannot be deployed into a card that will actually grade. The cost is not just the grading fee — it is the opportunity cost of the capital, the time in the grading queue (weeks to months depending on tier), and the psychological bandwidth of waiting for a result you could have predicted.

The Nograde Strategy is about one thing: ruthless honesty before submission. If a card cannot achieve a PSA 10, you sell it raw. Full stop.

---

## The four automatic nograde disqualifiers

Any one of these conditions — identified before submission — is an automatic nograde. Do not submit a card with any of these present:

**1. Centering outside 60/40**
For Prizm and Chrome cards, PSA requires 55/45 centering for a 10. A card that is visually off-center is almost certainly outside this tolerance. Measure with a ruler if you are unsure: measure the left border width and the right border width. If the ratio exceeds 60/40, it is a nograde.

**2. Soft or rounded corners**
Place the card face-down and examine each corner under raking LED light. A corner that appears slightly rounded rather than crisp and pointed is a soft corner — a PSA 9 at best. Four sharp, tight corners are non-negotiable for a 10.

**3. Edge chips or dings**
Run your fingertip slowly along each edge. Any nick, chip, or indentation is an edge flaw. Under LED light, hold the edge toward the light source and look along the edge plane — chips show up as shadows. Any visible edge damage is a nograde.

**4. Surface scratches visible under LED**
Hold the card at a 45-degree angle to a strong LED light source. Move the card slowly. Any scratches, scuffs, or print lines that catch the light are surface issues. Hairline scratches that are invisible under normal lighting are visible under LED and will cost you a grade. If you see any, it is a nograde.

---

## The secondary disqualifiers (context-dependent)

These are not automatic nogrades, but they require an honest assessment:

**Print defects:** Ink spots, print lines, color banding. Small print defects may be acceptable for older vintage cards where PSA grades more leniently on production issues. On modern Prizm/Chrome, any visible print defect is a grade killer.

**Staining:** Wax staining from vintage pack storage, paper staining from water or humidity. Staining that affects the surface is a nograde. Faint staining visible only under UV light is a judgment call.

**Creases:** Any crease — however faint — is a nograde for investment purposes. A barely-visible crease under LED light will be caught in grading.

## How to sell nogrades effectively

Identifying a nograde is only half the job. The other half is converting it to cash quickly so you can redeploy.

**Price raw cards accurately.** Search eBay for recent sales of the same card described honestly in its condition: "off-center," "light surface wear," "mid-grade raw." Raw buyers know what they're buying and price for it. Overpricing a nograde by pretending it is better than it is will result in no sales and stale inventory.

**Use condition-honest titles.** eBay buyers who specifically search for raw mid-grade copies exist and will pay fair prices. They are not looking for deception — they are looking for accurate information. A title like "2018 Prizm Luka RC Raw — light centering issue — see scans" will sell faster than a vague title that generates returns.

**Bundle when appropriate.** Low-value nogrades (under $30 each) can be bundled into lots by team, year, or set to reduce eBay fees and move inventory faster. A 10-card lot of raw mid-grade Prizm cards from a single team often sells faster and at better aggregate prices than individual listings.

**Set a 30-day rule.** Any nograde that has not sold in 30 days gets its price reduced by 20%. Stale inventory is dead capital. The loss on a small reduction is always less than the opportunity cost of holding.

## The math on holding vs. selling raw

A raw Mahomes 2017 Prizm with visible centering issues (off 65/35):
- Raw sale price: approximately $110
- If you grade it anyway: $110 (raw) + $50 (PSA fee) + $45 (shipping) = $205 in
- Expected grade: PSA 8 or below
- PSA 8 sale price: approximately $140
- Net result: $140 - $205 = **-$65 loss**

Versus selling raw at $110: **$110 recovered**, capital immediately available.

The nograde strategy is not about giving up. It is about deploying capital where it actually works.
