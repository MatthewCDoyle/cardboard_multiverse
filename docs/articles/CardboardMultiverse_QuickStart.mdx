## What Is This Spreadsheet?

The Cardboard Multiverse spreadsheet is a complete business system for card collectors — whether you flip one card a month or run a full dealer operation. It has 8 tabs, each built for a specific job. This guide walks you through every tab, tells you exactly what to enter, and explains what the formulas do automatically.

| Tab | What It Does | Who It's For |
|-----|-------------|--------------|
| 🏠 Welcome | Rules, color guide, navigation | Everyone — read first |
| 📊 Master Data | Benchmark gem rates & PSA 10 values | Reference — update monthly |
| 💰 Self-Sustaining | Track flips, auto-deduct eBay fees | Casual to mid-level flippers |
| 🔬 Grading ROI Analyst | Run the 2x Rule before you send to PSA | Graders & submitters |
| 🏛️ Empire Builder | SKU inventory with velocity & margin tracking | Dealers & power sellers |
| 🧭 Risk Dashboard | Monitor portfolio concentration limits | Serious collectors & investors |
| 📈 Charts | Auto-updating visual summaries | Everyone |
| 🎯 Prospector Scorecard | Score rookies before you buy | Prospect hunters |

## Before You Start — Colour Coding Key

Every cell in this spreadsheet follows a consistent colour system. Learn it once and you'll never be confused about what to do.

| Colour | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 🔵 **Blue text** | Input cell — this is where **you** type. No other cells need editing. |
| ⚫ **Black text** | Formula — calculated automatically. Do not overwrite. |
| 🟢 **Green** | Healthy / Pass / Profit / Safe — you're in good shape. |
| 🔴 **Red** | Warning / Fail / Loss / Over-concentrated — action needed. |
| 🟡 **Yellow** | Caution / Watch / Borderline — keep an eye on it. |
| 🟣 **Purple rows** | Alternating row highlight — no special meaning, just readability. |

## The Five Rules — Know These Cold

Every formula in this spreadsheet enforces one of these five rules. Understanding them makes the tool make sense.

<Callout type="info">
**✌️ The 2x Rule**

Your Target PSA 10 value must be **more than 2×** your total cost-in (raw card + grading fee). If the math doesn't work, don't grade it. The Grading ROI Analyst tab enforces this automatically.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
**⚠️ The 9 Penalty**

A PSA 9 is not a consolation prize — it's often a losing ticket. Your PSA 9 value must still cover your full cost-in or you lose money. The "9 Penalty" column on the Grading ROI Analyst tab flags this automatically.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
**📊 The 20/30/50/60 Rule**

No single card > 20% of portfolio. No single player > 30%. No single sport > 50%. No single era > 60%. The Risk Dashboard monitors all four automatically.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
**💸 The eBay Fee Rule**

Standard eBay fee is 13.25% of sale price. Always deduct this before counting profit. The Self-Sustaining tab does this automatically — your Net Profit column is already fee-adjusted.
</Callout>

<Callout type="error">
**⏱️ The 75-Day Rule**

If a card sits in your inventory for more than 75 days, re-price it or liquidate. Dead capital kills momentum. The Empire Builder tab turns velocity red at 75 days and yellow at 50 as an early warning.
</Callout>

## Tab-by-Tab Guide

### 📊 Master Data

This tab is a read-only reference. It shows PSA population data, gem rates, and current market values for 8 key benchmark cards. Use it to calibrate your intuition about what makes a card worth grading.

**What to do:**
- **Nothing on first use** — the data is pre-loaded from current PSA population reports.
- **Update monthly** — gem rates and target values shift as more cards get graded. Check PSA's population report and revise the numbers in column D (Gem Rate) and column E (Target PSA 10 $).

<Callout type="tip">
**💡 Pro Tip**

The Gem Rate column is the most important number on this tab. A card with a 0.008% gem rate (Mantle 1964) is a fundamentally different investment than one with a 64% gem rate (Wembanyama 2023). Low gem rate = high scarcity premium.
</Callout>

---

### 💰 Self-Sustaining Collector

This is your flip journal. Every buy and sell goes here. The sheet auto-calculates your net profit after eBay fees and allocates a portion to your Personal Collection (PC) fund.

**What to enter (blue cells only):**
- **Date** — when you acquired the card
- **Description** — card name, year, brand, grade
- **All-In Cost ($)** — everything you paid: card price, shipping, fees
- **Sale Price ($)** — your actual eBay (or other platform) selling price
- **PC Fund %** — what % of your profit to set aside for your personal collection. Default is 50%.

**What calculates automatically:**
- **Net Profit ($)** — Sale Price minus All-In Cost minus 13.25% eBay fee
- **PC Fund $** — your profit multiplied by your PC Fund %
- **Status** — shows 🔵 Listed while Sale Price is blank, then ✅ Sold once filled

<Callout type="tip">
**💡 Pro Tip**

Leave the Sale Price blank until the card actually sells. The Status column will show 🔵 Listed automatically, and your summary KPIs will only count completed sales.
</Callout>

---

### 🔬 Grading ROI Analyst

Run every card through the 2x Rule before you send it to PSA. This tab tells you whether a card is worth grading, and flags the hidden risk of receiving a PSA 9 instead of a 10.

**What to enter (blue cells only):**
- **Card Description** — name, year, set
- **Raw Cost ($)** — what you paid for the ungraded card
- **Grading Fee ($)** — current PSA tier fee — check [psacard.com](https://www.psacard.com) for current pricing
- **Target PSA 10 ($)** — current market value of a PSA 10 — check eBay sold listings
- **PSA 9 Value ($)** — current market value of a PSA 9 — check eBay sold listings

**What calculates automatically:**
- **Break-Even ($)** — Raw Cost + Grading Fee — your minimum to recover costs
- **2x Rule** — ✅ PASS if Target PSA 10 > 2× Break-Even, ❌ FAIL if not
- **9 Penalty** — ⚠️ LOSS RISK if PSA 9 value is below your Break-Even
- **ROI if PSA 10** — percentage return if you hit a 10
- **Net $ if PSA 10** — actual dollar profit after eBay fees if you hit a 10

<Callout type="warning">
**⚠️ Important**

A ❌ FAIL on the 2x Rule is a hard stop. Do not grade that card — you are paying PSA to gamble with your money. A ⚠️ LOSS RISK on the 9 Penalty means even a decent grade could lose you money. Both flags should override your gut feeling.
</Callout>

### 🏛️ Empire Builder

A full dealer-grade inventory management system. Every card gets a SKU, a velocity timer, and a margin calculation. Built for collectors running 20+ cards in inventory at a time.

**What to enter (blue cells only):**
- **Custom SKU** — your reference code, format: CMV-001, CMV-002, etc.
- **Player / Year / Brand** — full card description
- **Sourcing Channel** — where you bought it: eBay, LCS, COMC, show, etc.
- **Date Purchased** — enter as MM/DD/YY — the velocity timer starts from this date
- **Platform ID** — your listing ID on eBay, WhatNot, etc. for easy lookup
- **Status** — type `Active` or `Sold`
- **List Price ($)** — your asking price
- **Cost ($)** — your all-in purchase cost

**What calculates automatically:**
- **Velocity (Days)** — days since purchase — turns yellow at 50, red at 75
- **Gross Margin ($)** — List Price minus Cost
- **Margin %** — Gross Margin as a percentage of List Price — green if above 30%

<Callout type="tip">
**💡 Pro Tip**

Use the SKU system religiously. When you're managing 50+ cards, being able to search CMV-043 and instantly locate a card in your storage is worth more than any margin formula. Front-load the SKU on your storage boxes and shipping labels.
</Callout>

### 🧭 Risk Dashboard

Your portfolio health monitor. Update the five blue input cells and the dashboard tells you instantly whether your collection is dangerously over-concentrated in any one card, player, sport, or era.

**What to enter (blue cells — Portfolio Inputs section):**
- **Total Portfolio Value ($)** — add up the current market value of every card you own
- **Most Valuable Single Card ($)** — the current market value of your single most expensive card
- **Most-Held Player — Total Value ($)** — add up all cards of your most-concentrated player
- **Largest Sport Category Value ($)** — add up all cards in your biggest sport
- **Largest Era Category Value ($)** — add up all cards in your biggest era: vintage, junk wax, modern, ultra-modern

**What the dashboard shows:**
- **Your %** — each category as a percentage of your total portfolio
- **Status** — ✅ HEALTHY, ⚠️ WATCH, or 🚩 OVER-CONCENTRATED with colour coding
- **Recommendation** — plain-English explanation of the risk and what to do about it

<Callout type="tip">
**💡 Pro Tip**

Update this dashboard at the start of every month. Most collectors only discover they're over-concentrated after a bad grade or market dip has already hit. Five minutes of honest data entry here is your insurance policy.
</Callout>

### 📈 Charts

Two auto-updating charts that visualise your key data at a glance. No input needed — they pull directly from Master Data and the Risk Dashboard.

- **Chart 1: Gem Rate by Card** — shows how rare a PSA 10 is for each benchmark card. The Wembanyama bar dwarfs everything else — a reminder that ultra-modern is a fundamentally different product.
- **Chart 2: Portfolio Concentration vs. Safe Limits** — plots your actual concentration percentages against the 20/30/50/60 thresholds. Any bar exceeding the grey limit bar needs attention.

<Callout type="tip">
**💡 Pro Tip**

If you add new benchmark cards to Master Data, extend the chart data range manually (right-click the chart > Select Data) to include the new rows.
</Callout>

### 🎯 Prospector Scorecard

A structured scoring system for evaluating rookie cards before you buy. Score each prospect across six categories. The sheet calculates a total out of 100 and issues a verdict automatically.

**Scoring categories and maximums:**

| Category | Max | How to Score |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| Age at Debut | 20 | 20 = age 18–19 \| 15 = 20–21 \| 10 = 22–23 \| 5 = 24+ |
| Draft Pedigree | 20 | 20 = Top-5 pick \| 15 = Top-10 \| 10 = Top-30 \| 5 = other |
| Charisma / Swagger | 15 | 15 = elite marketability \| 10 = strong \| 5 = average \| 0 = unknown |
| Winning Pedigree | 15 | 15 = champion/titles \| 10 = deep playoff runs \| 5 = winning team |
| Statistical Upside | 20 | 20 = generational ceiling \| 15 = star \| 10 = starter \| 5 = fringe |
| Card Scarcity | 10 | 10 = short print / low pop \| 5 = moderate \| 0 = flooded |

**Verdict thresholds:**
- 🔥 **STRONG BUY (80–100)** — elite prospect, buy now
- ✅ **BUY (65–79)** — solid upside, worth the position
- 👀 **WATCHLIST (50–64)** — monitor — wait for a dip or more data
- ❌ **PASS (below 50)** — risk outweighs reward at current prices

<Callout type="warning">
**⚠️ Validation Note**

Score cells turn **red** if you enter a number above the maximum for that category, and **yellow** if you hit the exact maximum. The total will still calculate but a red cell means your score is invalid — re-check the criteria.
</Callout>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I add more rows?**

Yes. All formula columns (Net Profit, Status, Velocity, etc.) are pre-loaded for 200 rows in every tab. Just start typing in the next empty row.

**Can I change the eBay fee percentage?**

Yes. On the Self-Sustaining tab, the fee is hardcoded as `0.1325` (13.25%) in column F. If you sell on a different platform or have a different fee tier, find-and-replace `0.1325` with your actual rate.

**Can I add more benchmark cards to Master Data?**

Yes. Add new rows below the existing data in the same format. The Difficulty badge in column F needs to be typed manually (`🔴 EXTREME`, `🟠 HARD`, `🟡 MODERATE`, or `🟢 EASY`). Extend the Charts tab's data range manually if you want the new cards to appear in Chart 1.

**The Prospector Scorecard only goes to 100 — what if a card scores higher?**

It cannot. The maximum for each category is capped, and validation turns cells red if you exceed any limit. A score of 94–100 is genuinely rare and represents a generational talent.

**What if a card I'm tracking doesn't sell on eBay?**

The eBay fee formula is a shortcut — adjust the fee rate in column F of the Self-Sustaining tab to match your actual platform. WhatNot, COMC, and direct sales all have different fee structures.
