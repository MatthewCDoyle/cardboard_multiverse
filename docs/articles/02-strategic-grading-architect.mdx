## The problem with how most collectors approach grading

Most collectors approach grading as follows: find a card they like, decide it looks nice, submit it to PSA, hope for a 10.

This process has no analysis, no financial modeling, and no technical rigor. It works sometimes — and costs money most of the time.

The Strategic Grading Architect course rebuilds your approach from first principles: start with the market data, evaluate the card's physical condition against professional standards, run the financial math, and only submit when the model supports it.

---

## Module 1: How PSA Grading Actually Works

Most collectors think they understand PSA grading — they know the 1–10 scale. Far fewer understand how the grade is actually determined: the four dimensions (centering, corners, edges, surface), the tolerance standards at each grade level, how subgrades affect the final grade on qualifier cards, and how grader consistency (and inconsistency) affects the market.

This module demystifies the process so you can accurately predict grades rather than guessing.

**Key topics:** Centering tolerances by grade level, the holistic vs. subgrade grading debate, how PSA and BGS standards differ, consistency across grading tiers (Economy vs. Express vs. Walkthrough).

## Module 2: Reading and Calculating Population Reports

A deep-dive extension of the Pop Report how-to guide. This module adds: reading population trend data over time, identifying "bubble" populations (sets where a bulk submission recently inflated supply), cross-referencing BGS and SGC populations against PSA to understand true total market supply, and finding undervalued cards with unexpectedly low populations.

**Deliverable:** Pop Report Tracker Spreadsheet (100 key basketball and football cards, pre-loaded)

## Module 3: The LED Evaluation System

The in-depth version of the LED lighting how-to guide. This module includes video demonstrations — filmed under actual LED evaluation conditions — showing exactly how corners, edges, surfaces, and centering look at PSA 8, 9, and 10 grade levels.

The video component is what makes this module worth the course price on its own: seeing the difference between a soft corner and a sharp corner under LED light, on an actual card, eliminates the ambiguity that costs collectors money on bad submissions.

**Topics covered:** Centering measurement techniques, corner classification (sharp vs. soft vs. bent), edge evaluation under raking light, surface scratch identification by card type (chrome, foil, vintage cardstock), UV evaluation for vintage.

---

## Module 4: The Submission Strategy

Not all cards should go to PSA — and not all PSA submissions should use the same service tier. This module covers:

- Which submission tier is appropriate for each card value range
- How to batch submissions to minimize per-card shipping costs
- The submission form completion process (card description accuracy matters for grade disputes)
- How to handle a grade you disagree with (review process, reholder options)
- Cross-over submissions from BGS and SGC

**Deliverable:** Submission Tier Decision Matrix (PDF)

The final module synthesizes everything into an ongoing system. How to build a standing raw card inventory that feeds a regular submission schedule, how to set submission volume targets based on your capital, and how to track grading ROI across your entire submission history.

**Deliverable:** Grading Pipeline Tracker (spreadsheet — tracks raw cost, submission date, grade received, sale price, and net profit for every submission)
