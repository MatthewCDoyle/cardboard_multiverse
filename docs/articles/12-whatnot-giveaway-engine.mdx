## The giveaway is a marketing spend, not a loss

Most new Whatnot sellers view giveaways as a cost — cards given away with no revenue return. This framing is wrong.

Every follower who enables notifications on Whatnot receives a push notification to their phone when you go live. In a platform where the average live show has a 15–40 minute peak attention window, a buyer who receives a notification and tunes in is worth dramatically more over time than a one-time buyer you'll never see again.

The math: if a $10 card giveaway acquires 50 new notification-enabled followers, and those followers each make purchases averaging $35 across your next five shows, you generated $1,750 in follow-on revenue from a $10 giveaway. That is a 175x ROAS (return on ad spend) that no paid advertising channel can approach.

The giveaway is not charity. It is your lowest-cost customer acquisition channel.

---

## The notification conversion structure

The giveaway only produces value if it converts viewers to notification-enabled followers. The ask must be explicit, visible, and repeated.

**Script for the opening giveaway (within first 5 minutes):**

*"Welcome everyone — to kick off tonight's show I'm giving away this [card name] completely free, no purchase required. All you need to do is be following the show and have notifications on. I'm going to pull a winner in about two minutes using the randomizer — you can see it live on screen. If you're not following yet, follow now and hit the bell. The notifications are what let you know when I go live every [day of week], and I give away cards at the start of every show."*

Three elements are critical:
1. **No purchase required** — lowers the barrier to participation and signals good faith
2. **The explicit notification ask** — most viewers will not turn on notifications unless specifically asked
3. **The recurring show schedule** — establishes that this is an ongoing relationship, not a one-time show

## Giveaway card selection

The giveaway card should be:
- Recognizable to your audience (a well-known player, not a deep cut)
- Visually appealing (a Prizm or Chrome card with shine photographs well on stream)
- Priced at $8–$20 retail — valuable enough to feel meaningful, not so expensive that it eats your margin

**Do not give away your best cards.** The giveaway is the hook that opens the show. The best cards — your PSA 10 key assets — are the reason viewers stay and bid after the giveaway. The sequence is: giveaway draws them in → quality inventory keeps them engaged → bidding war on key cards generates revenue.

---

## The midpoint giveaway

Viewer counts on live shows peak early and drop off gradually. A second giveaway at the 45–60% point of your planned show duration re-engages viewers who have drifted and signals to the algorithm that your show has continued engagement.

The midpoint giveaway should be slightly higher value than the opening giveaway — this rewards viewers who stayed and creates incentive to stay through the end of the show. Announce it at the show's start: *"And at the midpoint tonight I've got [card name] for another giveaway for anyone who's been here with us."*

## Scheduling and notification optimization

Whatnot sends push notifications to your followers when you schedule a show 48–72 hours in advance and again when you go live. Schedule your shows consistently — same day and time each week — so followers develop a habit of tuning in.

Before each show, post on social media (Instagram, X, YouTube Community) to cross-promote. Your eBay store description and about page should mention your Whatnot show schedule with a direct link. Every channel should feed the notification list.

Track your follower count before and after every show. A show that adds 30+ followers is performing well. A show that adds fewer than 10 means either the giveaway card selection or the notification ask needs adjustment.
