## Why this set deserves a dedicated course

No other set in basketball collecting has the cultural weight, the condition complexity, and the authentication challenges of the 1986-87 Fleer Basketball set. The Jordan rookie alone has been the subject of more collector study than most entire sports.

This course is the most comprehensive resource available outside of direct dealer mentorship.

## Module 1: The 1986-87 Fleer Story

How the set was produced, distributed, and why it ended up so condition-sensitive. The cultural context of the 1986 NBA — the Bird/Magic rivalry, Jordan's emergence, the set of players who would define the decade. Why this specific year became the defining vintage basketball set rather than adjacent years.

## Module 2: Production Characteristics and Condition Vulnerabilities

Card-by-card analysis of which numbers in the set are most condition-sensitive and why. The centering tendencies by print sheet position. The specific defects — wax staining zones, surface print line patterns, corner softness tendencies — that affect specific card numbers more than others.

This module alone has prevented countless bad submissions and purchases for collectors who applied it.

## Module 3: The Key Cards Deep Dive

Detailed analysis of every card in the top two tiers: Jordan, Barkley, Olajuwon, Ewing, Malone, Drexler, Dumars, Magic, Bird. For each: current PSA population at every grade, gem rate calculation, recent comp history, condition tips specific to that card, and grading advice.

## Module 4: Authentication

The 1986 Fleer Jordan is the most frequently counterfeited basketball card in existence. This module covers: the authentication tells that separate genuine examples from fakes (paper stock characteristics, printing registration marks, color saturation), how to identify trimmed cards (cards with altered borders to improve centering grade), and how to evaluate authentication certificates from PSA and Beckett.

Video demonstrations under LED and UV lighting are included.

## Module 5: The Sticker Set

The 11 stickers are often treated as an afterthought. This module covers the full sticker set: production characteristics, condition vulnerabilities by sticker number, the Jordan sticker population data, and the strategy for completing the sticker set alongside or separately from the base set.

## Module 6: Acquisition Strategy

Three complete acquisition strategies mapped to three budget profiles: under $500 (raw commons + raw Jordan target), $500–$2,000 (raw complete set + PSA 8 Jordan), and $2,000+ (targeted graded acquisition across all key cards). For each budget tier: the exact sequence, the sourcing approach, and the patience requirements.
